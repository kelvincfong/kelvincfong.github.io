About

I am an environmental epidemiologist and current postdoctoral associate in Michelle Bell's Group at Yale University. Previously, I completed my doctorate in environmental health at Harvard University, where my advisor was Joel Schwartz .

My current research focuses on environmental health disparities. I am interested in how different population subgroups are affected by environmental exposures such as air pollution, heat, and green space. An exciting topic I am currently working on is how economic and racial segregation impacts population health.

I became interested in environmental epidemiology after research experiences in atmospheric chemistry and lung physiology during my undergraduate degree at University of Toronto. Following this passion, I completed my MSc in environmental sciences at ETH Zurich then worked at Verenum and the World Health Organization.
